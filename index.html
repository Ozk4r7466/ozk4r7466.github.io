Bio

Research built around household decisions and policy design.

My work uses experimental, quasi-experimental, and econometric methods to understand how energy reforms affect households. I focus on electricity pricing, subsidy design, energy insecurity, clean fuel choices, and behavioral messages such as peer comparison reports.

Beyond academic research, I have worked on evidence-based policy analysis for European institutions and international organizations. My teaching spans econometrics, statistics, microeconomics, environmental economics, and policy evaluation.

Research on how disruptions in electricity supply are linked with children's anthropometric outcomes and household well-being in the Kyrgyz Republic.
